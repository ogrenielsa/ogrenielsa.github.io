About me


Even during my childhood years, I remember taking pride in doing a good job. Working from such a young age, taught me how to be resilient, patient and responsible.

I have a lot of experience working in a variety of fields that had different requirements and working conditions. This has given me the opportunity to develop a wide range of skills that can be beneficial to any workspace.

Over the years, I have worked as an individual but also as part of a team, both as a waitress and a mechanical engineer, developing my communication and teamwork skills. I am always willing to listen to others' point of view, and I'm eager to learn from them, but I also stand strong by my opinion when I truly believe it's worthy.

I've also been working in fast-paced environments, like the ones present in the food industry and in the mechanical engineering world, that taught me how to cope with work stress and work efficiently under pressure.

When I started working as a mechanical engineer and managing large, expensive projects, I had to take bigger responsibilities and manage higher levels of stress. I was excited to be able to utilize my past skills: Managing customers, working with the team and making cost estimations. But also, I had to develop new ones, required for the design and machine-operating parts of the job.

Another side of the job that I loved, was that the company wasn't a behemoth, so I was able to take responsibilities in different sections and get in touch with every single aspect of it. Starting as a junior mechanical design engineer, I was promoted to the position of General Manager. I had to learn how to manage the company, the team, the customers and our stock.

I have a growth mindset; and I learned a lot the last years, but I'm ready for a new challenge.
